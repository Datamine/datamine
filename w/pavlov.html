Pavlovian Triggers for Productivity

In 1901, the Russian physiologist Ivan Pavlov ran a set of experiments on dogs. He would ring a bell, and then present the dogs with food. After establishing this pattern, he rang the bell, and then did not present the dogs with food, but found that they nonetheless salivated as if they were about to eat. Pavlov concluded that when there exists a pair of physical stimulus and response (e.g. food as stimulus and salivation as response), it is possible to teach an association between the physical stimulus and an unrelated stimulus (e.g. a ringing bell), such that even when the physical stimulus is removed, the unrelated stimulus still provokes the physical response. This is called classical conditioning and it is one of the staples of modern psychology.
